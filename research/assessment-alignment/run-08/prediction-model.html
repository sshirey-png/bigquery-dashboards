Executive Summary

Key Finding
Two predictors explain 72% of LEAP Math variance and 59% of LEAP ELA variance: anet BOY + PM aggregate. Adding a third predictor (ORF, grades, MAP) gains less than 4 percentage points of R² for ELA and less than 1pp for Math. The two-predictor model is the recommended operational tool.
Key Finding
Model-based risk tiers catch 93–97% of future Unsatisfactory students with a false positive rate of only 0.7–3.9%. This dramatically outperforms the Run 5 simple tiers (anet BOY alone), which caught only 80–83%. The model nearly eliminates missed at-risk students.
Key Finding
PM is the single most important predictor in every subject model. PM Math alone (R²=.697) outperforms anet Math BOY (R²=.481), MAP Math percentile (R²=.703 but 26% coverage), and course grades (R²=.169). PM was undervalued in Runs 1–5.
Key Finding
All models validated with 70/30 train-test splits show minimal overfitting: train–test R² gap is ≤0.023 across all subjects. The Math model actually performs better on the test set (R²=.724 test vs .717 train), confirming robust generalization.
Null Finding
Course grades add near-zero predictive value. After accounting for anet BOY + PM, adding course grades gains ΔR²=.0065 for ELA and .0005 for Math. Grades are informationally redundant once standardized assessments are included.

Operational Recommendations

  1. Deploy the two-predictor models for ELA and Math. They require only anet BOY (September) and cumulative PM scores. Both are already collected network-wide. No new data collection is needed.
  2. Deploy the three-predictor PM models for Science and Social Studies. These subjects have no anet or MAP assessments; PM is the only internal predictor system. Cross-subject PM signals (ELA reading, Math reasoning) add 6–13 percentage points of R².
  3. Run the model after each PM assessment cycle. PM scores accumulate throughout the year. Re-running the prediction after each major PM assessment window will refine predictions as more data becomes available.
  4. Use the September anet BOY score for initial risk flagging. anet BOY alone catches 81–83% of Unsatisfactory students. This is the earliest available signal. Supplement with the full model once PM data is available.
  5. Do not use course grades for LEAP prediction. Grades add <0.01 R² after accounting for anet and PM. They are informationally redundant for this purpose.
  6. Expand MAP Math BOY administration. If MAP Math were available for all students (currently 26%), the Math model would reach R²=.802. This is the single highest-value data collection improvement available.
