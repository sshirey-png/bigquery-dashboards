Synthesis & Recommendations

KEY FINDING
Science and Social Studies have no dedicated early-warning systems, but they don't need one. anet ELA BOY predicts both LEAP Science (r=0.614) and Social Studies (r=0.635) at levels that support actionable risk identification. The existing anet ELA early-warning threshold (<30%) should be treated as a pan-subject risk flag.
KEY FINDING
Number sense domains (Base Ten, Fractions, Number System) explain 49–58% of LEAP Math variance—far more than procedural domains (Measurement & Data at 29%, Statistics at 12%). Strengthening number sense instruction would yield the highest return on LEAP Math outcomes.
KEY FINDING
Reading Informational Text is the single most broadly predictive domain in the network. It correlates r ≥ 0.60 with LEAP ELA, Math, Science, and Social Studies. It is the one domain where improvement would lift all four LEAP subjects.
