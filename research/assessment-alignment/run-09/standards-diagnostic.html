9 Methodology Notes

  • Data source: Progress Monitoring (PM) item-level responses from PowerSchool Performance Matters, linked to LEAP 2025 scale scores and subscores via State Student Number.
  • Correlation method: Pearson product-moment correlation between student-level PM percent correct on each standard and LEAP scale scores / subscores.
  • Minimum sample: Standards required n ≥ 50 matched students to be included in correlation analysis.
  • Deduplication: 56 PS.Math.Content standards are exact mirrors of CCSS.Math.Content standards. Where both exist, the PS version is excluded to avoid double-counting.
  • Quadrant thresholds: Difficulty < 50% average correct = “hard”; r ≥ 0.5 = “predictive”. These thresholds were chosen based on distributional breakpoints.
  • School-level gaps: Weighted network average computed from individual school n-counts. Cells flagged when >10 percentage points below network mean.
  • Weakness profiles: A student is “weak” on a standard if their average percent correct < 50%. Only standards tested for each student are counted.
  • Caveat: Correlations reflect association, not causation. PM item quality, curricular alignment, and timing of assessments may influence observed relationships.
  • Enrichment subject: Excluded from analysis due to insufficient sample size (n = 2 standards with correlations).
