Executive Headlines

(KEY FINDING) Performance Matters is the strongest LEAP predictor in the network — stronger than anet interims. PM Math correlates with LEAP Math at r=0.829 (n=1,883), exceeding anet Math BOY (r=0.687), anet Math MOY (r=0.724), and rivaling MAP Math percentile (r=0.840) with 3.7× the coverage. PM English correlates at r=0.753 (n=1,883) vs anet ELA EOY at r=0.667.
(KEY FINDING) PM provides the ONLY internal predictors for Science and Social Studies. PM Science → LEAP Science: r=0.714 (n=1,876). PM Social Studies → LEAP Social Studies: r=0.741 (n=1,881). No other internal system (anet, MAP, grades) assesses these subjects.
(CORRECTION) Run 6 reported "zero Social Studies data" in Performance Matters. This was wrong. PM has 232,670 Social Studies rows across 2,453 students in 2024-25. The Subject value is "Social Stu" (truncated string), not "Social Studies." The Run 6 query searched for the wrong string.
(KEY FINDING) On the same students, PM outperforms anet as a LEAP predictor. Head-to-head on n=1,844 students with both PM and anet data: PM ELA r=0.756 vs anet ELA r=0.733 (+0.023); PM Math r=0.830 vs anet Math r=0.765 (+0.065).
(KEY FINDING) ORF (oral reading fluency) is a moderate ELA predictor for grades 3–8 (r=0.563, n=1,596) but substantially weaker than PM or anet. ORF adds no value beyond existing assessments for LEAP prediction.
(KEY FINDING) Cross-year validation confirms stable anet-LEAP correlations. 23-24 anet BOY → 23-24 LEAP: ELA r=0.640 (n=1,741), Math r=0.735 (n=1,709). These replicate the 24-25 values (ELA r=0.633, Math r=0.687) within sampling error, confirming the relationships are structural, not artifacts of a single cohort.
(NULL FINDING) PM Math Quizzes (r=0.829) predict LEAP Math better than PM Math Tests (r=0.762) or Standards Checkpoints (r=0.746). Quizzes aggregate more frequently and across more content — their signal emerges from breadth, not rigor.

ORF benchmark status has poor sensitivity (55.3%) — it misses nearly half of LEAP-proficient students. For comparison, the anet BOY <30% threshold catches 81-83% of Unsatisfactory students (Run 3). ORF is a weaker screening tool than anet for LEAP risk identification.

AIMSWeb Coverage (2024-25, Grades 3–8)

AIMSWeb has strong grade 4–8 coverage (269–309 students per grade across all 4 schools) and includes Fall/Winter/Spring benchmark periods. This fills the ORF Grade 4 gap. However, AIMSWeb scores were not correlated with LEAP in this run — the AIMSWeb table uses a different student identifier (StudentLastName_StudentFirstName) that would require fuzzy matching to link to LEAP via LASID.

Schema Gotchas & Dead Ends

New Schema Gotchas (Run 7)

SeverityFinding
CRITICALPM Subject "Social Stu" is a truncated string, NOT "Social Studies." Run 6 searched for the wrong value and incorrectly reported zero SS data. Always use Subject LIKE 'Social%' or exact match 'Social Stu'.
CRITICALPM n values inflate dramatically if you don't INNER JOIN to LEAP first. PM covers K-8; LEAP tests only 3-8. A query returning n=3,449 for PM Math includes ~1,566 students with no LEAP data.
CRITICALPM Assessment_Category includes literal \N (backslash-N string), not SQL NULL. Must filter: Assessment_Category != r'\N' in BigQuery (raw string) or Assessment_Category IS NOT NULL AND Assessment_Category != '\\N'.
CRITICALORF Grade 4 has only 33 students in orf_data_combined (24-25). AIMSWeb covers ~269 Grade 4 students but uses student name identifiers (no LASID), preventing direct LEAP correlation.
CRITICAL23-24 LEAP SocialScaleScore is NULL for all records across all 4 schools. Social Studies LEAP was either not administered or not loaded for 23-24.
WARNINGAmplify data covers only Grade 8 (n=319). The DIBELS 8th Ed Composite underperforms raw ORF alone (r=0.513 vs 0.587 for ELA).
WARNINGAIMSWeb uses student name as identifier (StudentLastName_StudentFirstName), not LASID or Student_Number. Joining to LEAP requires fuzzy name matching — unreliable.
WARNINGORF data for School_Year=2024 may overlap with School_Year=2023 depending on enrollment dates. The School_Year field appears derived from enrollment, not test date.
INFOeasyCBM dataset not found under any naming variant. May have been removed or renamed.
INFODIBELS combined view (dibels_data_combined_acl) requires Drive credentials — inaccessible. Use Amplify benchmark table instead for DIBELS 8th Ed data.
