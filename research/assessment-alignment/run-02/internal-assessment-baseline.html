Headline Findings

(KEY FINDING) Massive grade inflation in Math across the network. At Wheatley 6th grade, 64.7% of students earn ≥80% course grades but score <40% on anet Math interims (n=85). At Ashe 6th grade: 57.3% (n=89). At LHA 4th grade: 56.8% (n=81). Course grades and external assessments are measuring fundamentally different things.
(KEY FINDING) anet and MAP agree strongly on ELA (r = 0.63–0.73) but disagree on Math (r = 0.13–0.48). At LHA, the Math anet–MAP BOY correlation is essentially zero (r = 0.13, n=145). These instruments may be measuring different math constructs, or coverage gaps are distorting the signal.
(KEY FINDING) anet BOY scores cleanly separate future LEAP levels. Students who will score Unsatisfactory on LEAP average 21% on anet BOY; those who will score Mastery average 57–64%. The separation is monotonic and consistent across ELA and Math. anet interims are useful early-warning tools.
(NULL FINDING) anet ELA scores DROP from BOY to MOY, contradicting MAP growth. Across all four schools, anet ELA averages decline 3–7 percentage points BOY→MOY, while MAP RIT scores grow +2 to +8 points over the same window. This means either (a) anet MOY is harder than BOY, or (b) students genuinely decline between fall and winter on the construct anet measures. Not necessarily contradictory—but a signal for curriculum leaders to investigate.
(KEY FINDING) MAP Math BOY coverage is only 28.8% of anet Math students. Only 506 of 1,757 anet Math students have MAP Math BOY scores. All Math concordance results (anet–MAP correlation, MAP–LEAP correlation) should be interpreted with extreme caution due to this coverage gap.
(NULL FINDING) Ashe 7th grade Math: grades ANTI-predict anet performance. Correlation between Y1 Math course grade and anet EOY Math: r = −0.17 (n=99). Students with higher grades score lower on external assessments. This is the most extreme grade-inflation signal in the dataset.

anet and MAP show opposite growth directions in ELA. anet scores decline 3–7 pp while MAP RIT scores increase 2–8 points.
